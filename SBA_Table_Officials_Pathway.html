Becoming a table official

The game runs through you.

Every basketball game needs a scorer and a timer at the table. It's one of the quickest ways into the sport, the lowest-barrier qualification to earn, and for many officials it's the start of a real journey — right up to the National League.

Two roles, one table

What a table official actually does.

At every CVL game there's a scorer and a timer sat side by side. You'll start with one role, learn the other, and eventually work both comfortably.

Role 01

The scorer

Keeps the official record of the game — who scored, how many, when they fouled, and which team has possession. In the Surrey CVL we use e-scoring through PlayHQ, so it's all done on a tablet rather than paper.

  • Record every basket, free throw and foul in PlayHQ
  • Track running score and player fouls for both teams
  • Signal the referees when a player reaches foul-out
  • Manage the possession arrow between plays
Role 02

The timer

Runs the game clock — starting, stopping and resetting it in response to the referees' whistle and the action on court.

  • Operate the game clock across four quarters
  • Run the timeout and half-time clocks
  • Sound the horn for substitutions and quarter ends
  • Coordinate closely with the scorer throughout
Why referee with Surrey

We don't just hand you a whistle and hope for the best.

Refereeing is hard, especially at first. So we built a programme where no new official is ever left on their own, and every referee has a clear route to the next level.

01

Mentored, not abandoned

Every newly qualified referee starts with a dedicated coach on site. You'll get feedback between quarters and a written note after every session — not just "good game" and a payslip.

02

A real progression route

Four structured tiers take you from U10 development games through to U18 senior junior competition, and on towards the Basketball England Level 3 award.

03

Paid from game one

You earn match fees from your very first fixture. As you progress through the age groups, fees increase — up to £25 a game at U16 and U18.

Why start at the table

The quickest way into officiating basketball.

You don't need to have played. You don't need to blow a whistle. You need to pay attention, learn the e-scoresheet, and you're in.

01

Qualified in an afternoon

The Basketball England Level 1 Table Official course takes about three hours — two hours of online modules and a one-hour scoresheet assessment. No playing experience needed.

02

Tablet, not paper

Surrey CVL uses e-scoring through PlayHQ. You score on a tablet and results flow straight to the league table — much more intuitive than a traditional paper scoresheet.

03

A doorway to more

Many of our table officials go on to become referees, progress to the Junior NBL, or reach the National League table. The pathway starts right here.

The pathway in detail

Four tiers. One path.

Click through each tier to see where you'd referee, who mentors you, and what you need to move up.

01
Your first games, with someone in your corner.
For anyone brand new to officiating, or anyone still building confidence after their Level 1 course.

What happens

You start with our pre-season tournament — short games in a low-pressure environment, with Miguel coaching you through the experience as it happens. You'll get structured written feedback on what you did well and two or three things to work on.

From there, you're deployed to U10 CVL games, where Miguel is on site every session as your mentor. As you find your feet, you'll be encouraged to step up to U12 games at the same venue — still within reach of your coach, but more demanding on the whistle.

What you're working on

  • Basic mechanics, positioning and signalling
  • Staying calm when coaches ask questions
  • Working confidently with your co-official and the table
  • Surrey-specific rules: passerelle, zone defence restrictions, full-court defence

02
Faster games, bigger kids, higher standards.
For referees who've found their rhythm at U10 and U12 and are ready for the jump to U14.

What happens

Your home is U14 games, where our second referee coach Max is based every session. The game is faster, the players are bigger, and you'll be managing bench behaviour more actively — so the support on the sidelines steps up to match.

During this tier you'll be preparing for the Basketball England Level 2 course. Two of your three BE Level 2 assessment games can actually be completed inside the CVL itself — meaning your progression happens in the league you already know.

What you're working on

  • Game management at a higher tempo
  • The 20-point rule and zone defence restrictions at U14
  • Pre-game and half-time communication with your co-official
  • Reading bench behaviour and stepping in early

03
Full-court mechanics. FIBA rules. Real stakes.
For qualified Level 2 referees taking on senior junior competition.

What happens

You're now refereeing U16 games, with U18 on the horizon. Both age groups run on full FIBA rules with two-person mechanics — lead and trail positions, rotations, and full-court coverage. Miguel and Max attend these sessions on rotation to coach you through the transition.

For referees wanting extra development, we also appoint officials to senior men's and women's CVL games. Level 2 referees are preferred, though experienced Level 1s who have shown real progress may also be appointed. The adult tempo, more vocal benches, and full-regulation format are excellent preparation for Level 3 assessment work.

What you're working on

  • Managing games with real competitive stakes

The pathway in detail

Four tiers. One path.

Click through each tier to see which role you'd start on, which age groups you'd cover, and what you need to move up.

01
One role at a time. Experienced partner beside you.
For anyone brand new to the table — including under-16s doing their very first sessions.

What happens

You start on a single role — usually the scoresheet, because it's where most people's confidence grows fastest — alongside an experienced table official who already knows both jobs. You'll work U10 and U12 games where the tempo is manageable and there's space to think.

The Commissioner at each CVL session keeps an eye on new table officials and is there to answer questions between games. Because we use PlayHQ e-scoring, you won't need to learn a paper scoresheet to get started — you'll pick up the digital version as you go, with the experienced official walking you through it.

What you're working on

  • Completing the PlayHQ e-scoresheet accurately
  • Tracking running score, fouls and possession arrow
  • Operating the game clock in time with the referee's whistle
  • Communicating clearly with your partner at the table

Comfortable with both roles. Ready for faster games.
For officials who can work the scoresheet and the clock with confidence, and are ready to step up.

What happens

You'll switch between scorer and timer as needed across U12 and U14 games, and start being appointed to U16 fixtures too. The tempo is quicker, fouls accumulate faster, and good communication with the referees and your table partner really matters.

This is also when you start preparing for the BE Level 2 Table Official course. It requires four assessment games — two as scorer and two as timer — and two of those four can be completed in approved CVL fixtures. Your progression happens inside the league you already know.

What you're working on

  • Switching fluidly between scorer and timer roles
  • Handling the faster pace of U14 and U16 games
  • Running timeouts, substitutions and quarter breaks cleanly

04
Into the national pathway.
For experienced Level 2 officials progressing towards the BE Level 3 award.

What happens

Basketball England requires two full seasons at Level 2 across the scorer and timer roles before you can enrol on the Level 3 course. Surrey is where you build that depth of experience — officiating our highest-intensity games and mentoring the table officials coming up behind you.

Level 3 introduces a third role: the shot clock operator. We don't currently use a shot clock in the CVL — our referees manage the time rule verbally — but the committee is reviewing whether to purchase one to support officials in training. For now, shot clock operation is learned through the Level 3 course itself and applied at NBL fixtures.

A qualified Level 3 table official can work all NBL competitions and below. At Surrey, Tier 4 officials continue to support CVL finals day and are role models for the rest of the programme.

What your role looks like

  • Officiating CVL finals day and the highest-tempo senior games
  • Preparing for Junior and senior NBL appointments
  • Learning the shot clock role through the Level 3 course
  • Supporting the development of newer table officials

Getting started

What you need to officiate in Surrey.

Before your first game, there are a handful of essentials to put in place. We'll guide you through each of them.

1

BE Level 1 qualification

The Basketball England Level 1 course is the starting point. It covers basic rules, mechanics and signals, and takes around six hours including a practical session.

2

BE membership & licence

You'll need an active Basketball England membership and a referee licence at the appropriate level to officiate any CVL game.

3

Safeguarding checks

If you're 16 or over, you'll need a DBS check and a safeguarding self-declaration. Over 18s also need a safeguarding qualification. We help you through the process.

4

Register with SBA

We'll register you on PlayHQ and add you to our referee WhatsApp group so you can pick up Sunday appointments across the September to April season.

Getting started

What you need to officiate in Surrey.

The table official route has the lowest barrier to entry in basketball. Here's everything you need.

1

BE Level 1 qualification

The Basketball England Level 1 Table Official course is about three hours long — two hours of online modules, one hour of scoresheet assessment. No prior experience needed.

2

BE membership & licence

Once you've passed the course, you'll need an active Basketball England membership and a table official licence to officiate any CVL game.

3

Safeguarding checks

If you're 16 or over, you'll need a DBS check and a safeguarding self-declaration. Over 18s also need a safeguarding qualification. We'll help you through the process.

4

Register with SBA

We'll register you on PlayHQ and add you to our officials group so you can pick up Sunday appointments across the September to April season.
